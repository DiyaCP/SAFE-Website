Sexual Harassment Laws

Sexual harassment is a punishable offense under various laws in India. Below are the key laws and guidelines that protect individuals from sexual harassment:

  • The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition, and Redressal) Act, 2013: This law mandates the establishment of an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in every workplace to address complaints of sexual harassment.
  • Indian Penal Code (Section 354, 509): These sections criminalize acts of outrage of modesty and insulting the modesty of women through words, gestures, or actions.
  • Vishaka Guidelines: Established by the Supreme Court, these guidelines laid the foundation for addressing workplace sexual harassment before the 2013 Act was implemented.

For more details, visit the official website of the Ministry of Women and Child Development.
